//01 About

small team. big problems.

exempli is a small bench of senior engineers who care about software that fits. We build custom — not because it's fashionable, but because most teams have outgrown the off-the-shelf tools they started with.

//02 Mission

We exist to help teams replace the half-dozen apps that almost work with one thing that fits — designed around how they actually run, maintained by the people who built it.

//03 What we believe
001 · Bias to fit

Software designed around your team, not a generic best practice.

Off-the-shelf is optimized for the average customer. You're not the average. The fit matters more than the feature list.

002 · Less stack

Fewer tools, tighter integration, lower mental load.

Every app you adopt is a tax on attention. We build the one thing that quietly replaces three of them.

003 · Stay on

We maintain what we ship. No throwaway demos.

Production-grade from day one. We're around when reality hits the model, not in a deck six weeks ago.

//04 Founders

Ethan Walker

Co-founder · engineer · architect

Ethan co-founded exempli with Charlie after watching one too many teams pay for five tools that half-solved one problem. He's built systems for clinics, logistics startups, indie studios, and solo founders — always with the same bias: ship the one thing that actually fits.

Outside of exempli he tinkers with synths, writes the occasional essay, and is a reliable second opinion on whether your stack really needs that fourth dashboard.

Charlie Thompson

Co-founder · AI engineer · data architect

Charlie runs the AI and data side of exempli. He's built training pipelines for clinical decision-support models, real-time fraud detection for fintech, and the kind of data infrastructure that doesn't fall over when someone runs a backfill at 2am. He cares more about evals than benchmarks, and more about feedback loops than feature counts.

Charlie believes most teams need fewer models, not more — and that the boring parts (data quality, monitoring, retrieval that actually returns the right thing) are where the real leverage lives. Outside exempli he reads too many ML papers and keeps threatening to publish his own.
